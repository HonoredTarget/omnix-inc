Chamblee's Engineering Clubs

Vex Robotics

Chamblee Vex Robotics exist to encourage and inspires students at Chamblee Charter High Schools to pursue experiences and opportunities in STEM through robotics that will instill skills in teamwork, research, design, collaboration, problem solving, critical thinking and building in project-based learning. "The VEX Robotics Competition, presented by the Robotics Education & Competition Foundation, is the largest and fastest growing middle school and high school robotics program globally with more than 20,000 teams from 50 countries playing in over 1,700 competitions worldwide. Each year, an exciting engineering challenge is presented in the form of a game. Students, with guidance from their teachers and mentors, build innovative robots and compete year-round."

NSBE Jr.

Chamblee NSBE Jr. is a vital and vibrant club that exist to expose, encourage and inspire students to pursue STEM experiences and opportunities that will prepare them to be leaders and global citizens that fulfill the mission of NSBE which is to increase the number of culturally responsible Black engineers that excel academically, succeed professionally and positively impact the community. For more information on NSBE Jr. go to: NSBE.org.

SHPE

The Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers Jr (SHPE Jr) at Chamblee Charter HS exist to promote and encourage students to pursue opportunities and experiences in STEM that will help them to build skills in areas such as teamwork, problem solving, critical thinking, project based learning that will equip them to become leaders and productive members of a global technological society. SHPE is a national organization whose mission is to changes lives by empowering the Hispanic community to realize its fullest potential and to impact the world through STEM awareness, access, support and development. SHPE's vision is a world where Hispanics are highly valued and influential as the leading innovators, scientists, mathematicians and engineers.
